## Helical Gear Geometry
The forces on a helical gear resolve themselves into three orthogonal axses. There are two anlges now: the pressure angle $\phi$ and the helix and $\psi$. 

To do our typical force analysis, we have to transform the gear into a form that is more similar to helical gears.

Transverse pitch:
$$
P_{t}=P_{n}\cos(\psi)
$$
This is the pitch looking at the helical gear laying flat. The forces that exist in the transverse plane are usually the ones we care about for the [[Stresses In Gears]].
## Stresses In Helical Gears
The geometry factor $J$ has an additional dependence for helical gears which is the helix angle $\psi$.
> The optimal helix angle is $\psi=15\text{degrees}$.

Addiotionally the geometry factor $I$ will also be modified depending on the gear ratio between the gears.
