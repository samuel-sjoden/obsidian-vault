The generalized uncertainty principle is as follows:
$$
\Delta A\Delta B\geq\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{ \braket{ i[\hat{A},\hat{B}] } ^2 }
$$
This tells you that if you have two operators that are not commutative, then you will always have some uncertainty in the other domain. If your operators are commutative, then knowing one observable, it is possible to determine the other observable with absolute precision.
