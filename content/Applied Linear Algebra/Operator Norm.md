The operator norm of a matrix $A$ descibes the maximum amount that an operator can increase the norm of the its input.

$$
\left| \left| A \right|  \right| = \text{max}_{x \neq 0} \frac{\left| \left| Ax \right|  \right| }{\left| \left| x \right|  \right| }
$$
If $A$ is non-singular we can replace the arbitrary $x$ with a unit vector and now it is describing the maxium stretch that $A$ can apply to the unit vector.
