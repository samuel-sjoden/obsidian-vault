The codomain is a space which contains the subspace of which a linear transform maps to. 

For example:
$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 0 \\
2 & 4 & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Is a mapping from $\mathbb{R}^3$ to $\mathbb{R}^2$. $\mathbb{R}^2$ is a codomain of the linear transform since it contains all of the possible elements that $A$ can map to. 
> [!Important]
> It is **not** what $A$ maps to. It can contain elements that $A$ can not map to
