The null space is a subspace of the domain of $A$. It tells you which vectors in the domain get mapped to zero. This tells you that if $x \in N(A)$ then it is [[Orthogonal]] to the row space of $A$.

That is if $$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
a_{1}^{T} \\
\dots \\
a_{m}^{T}
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Where $a$ are the row vectors of the matrix; $a$ are the equations in the system,
then $x$ is orthogonal to each equation/vector.
$\left< x,a \right> = 0$. 

### Solutions to $Ax=b$
If the null space of the matrix $A$ is greater than 1, this means that not all solutions are unique
