In the context of linear algebra, the domain is the set of all possible inputs to the linear mapping $A$.
> [!Example]
> The matrix A
> $$
 A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
 3 & 4 \\
 5 & 6
\end{bmatrix}$$
> Maps from $\mathbb{R}^2$ to $\mathbb{R}^3$. Its domain is $\mathbb{R}^2$ because it can take any element of $\mathbb{R}^2$ as its input
