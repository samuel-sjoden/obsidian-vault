## Boundary Conditions
IF we have a surface with some electric field passing through it. In three space two components are parallel and one is normal to the surface. We want to determine of the $\vec{E}$ is continuous across the boundary or if there is a jump.

Recall that electric field can be discontinuous.

$$
\nabla \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_{0}}
$$
$$
\nabla cross \vec{E}= 0
$$
1. The parallel components of the $\vec{E}$ is continuous across a charged boundary
2. The perpendicular component of $\vec{E}$ is discontinuous across a charged boundary

	If we have a surface charge density $\sigma$ , we ca use a Gaussian pillbox to fund the perpendicular component of the $\vec{E}$ field.

The divergence of the electric field at the surface is non-zero so there is an electric field.
