Linear [[Magnitude and Phase Response|Phase Shifts]] apply the same delay at all frequencies. We often approximate phase shift to be linear by applying a [[Taylor Expansion]] for:
$$
\angle H(j\omega) = \phi(\omega) \approx \phi(\omega_{0}) + (\omega-\omega_{0})\phi'(\omega_{0}) + \dots
$$
We can approximate the phase shift for a small range to be $$
\begin{align}  \\
\phi = \phi(\omega_{0}) \\
\alpha = \frac{d\phi(\omega_{0})}{dt}
\end{align}
$$
Where alpha is the **Group Delay**. It decribes how phase shift changes with frequencies.  This is helpful when drawing [[Bode Plots]].
